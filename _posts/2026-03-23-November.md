Apparently, it's the time for memoir. Maybe the peak of this time has passed, seeing that the memoir as a genre hits bestseller lists popular. Memoirist Vivian Gornick says it's partly historical. Since the sixties, we have been giving testimonies as a culture-as activitists for civil rights, feminism, the environment, etc.-which carried over to the personal space. This explains the tendency for memoirs to be about family or personal secrets and abuse. 

[This](/assets/james_november.pdf) is the work sample I wrote for my MFA application, part of what I hope will one day be a memoir, one, however, that looks as much to the Other and to God as much as it does to the self.
