<h1 style="font-size: 2.8em; font-family: Helvetica Neue; margin-bottom: 0.5em;">
  Building of Beauty
</h1>

<p style="font-size: 1.05em; font-style: italic; color: #666;">
  May 2, 2026
</p>

Jayden Choi

I woke up in the bed opposite to my parents on the ten-day trip in Spain. I still felt the recoil from watching cartoons on the television the night before. I crawled into the bathroom sluggishly and almost fell asleep with my toothbrush still in my mouth. Then, my parents began to wake up and started to get ready for our tour of Antoni Gaudí’s architecture in Barcelona. 

The morning cartoons were painful to watch since they were targeted toward a much younger audience, so I started to drift asleep again until my dad told me to look up the Sagrada Familia while they were getting ready. Mumbling things when he was out of earshot, I searched it up and found that it was a cathedral still under construction. It got a bit boring after a few seconds, and while I tried to snap back awake, my eyelids drooped down and the bed felt so warm and comfortable. After my dad woke me up and took his phone back, I was dragged out to breakfast. 

When we finally entered the world outside, there was a striking contrast to the quiet, cozy hotel room. Horns blaring, people yelling, and pigeons squawking. My parents hauled me into a bakery where I nibbled on a croissant with more air in it than a potato chip bag. My dad, our planner, walked us toward the plaza where we would meet up with the tour group. I slouched on a bench while the others gathered. Even when our guide lended us earbuds connected to his microphone, I couldn’t hear over the ruckus of morning Barcelona. So, I tuned out most of their tour-plan explanations.

I was having quite a wonderful time on the bench when the group started toward a structure. Since I was only just starting to gain consciousness, I didn't listen to the explanation they shared on the way. It also didn't help that our guide was speaking in Korean, which is my second language, and I would have preferred English. We finally came to a halt at a building that I could view at my own pace. It seemed full of turtle skull balconies and a weird lizard on the roof. After more talking, everyone walked into the building, which was called Casa Batlló , and we came to a staircase with a flat glass railing. 

“Did you hear that?” my mom asked, startling me.

I tried to cut off any further discussion with a sleepy “yep,” but she continued.

“The guide told us that if we record a video while walking along this glass it looks like water flowing!”

She made me record the glass, and it mimicked a wave in the ocean. The kaleidoscopic movements were surprisingly realistic, but, tired, I just gave a grunt in return.

When we moved onto our next attraction the lack of sleep, food, and water was taking its toll on me. Casa Milá, the alleged attraction, was mostly shaped like any other hotel, but it did have pointy, hard-to-make-out objects on the roof. The drone of the guide wasn’t intriguing enough for me to care and my legs were giving out. Exhausted, I sat on the floor. I was told that the floor was dirty so I should get up, but I stood my ground. Soon after, it was lunchtime, and the guide said our tour would continue in about forty minutes.
My dad asked, “Do you remember the sushi place we walked by yesterday?”


I reluctantly agreed to go, trying to look disappointed.

We walked about a block to our destination and placed our orders. The food took too long to come out so I drank a gallon of water before I even ate a morsel. Although the water made me feel full, I had to force some slimy fish down my throat or I would be famished during the remainder of the tour. I was feeling satisfied right before the tour restarted, but after I took a visit to the bathroom and flushed I felt hungry again. If I held my mouth I would be spared from an “I told you so,” lecture, so I said nothing.

As the tour continued, we neared the main attraction, the Sagrada Familia. The guide tried teaching us about the cathedral, but all I heard was blah blah blah, something about creating it for everyone, blah blah blah. As we began to close in on the cathedral the guide told us to look down and to not look up until the whole cathedral was visible. After walking for what felt like ten minutes our guide told us that the tour was over and that we could look up. 

In what seemed like a flash I ran to the front of the gates. I walked into the mesmerizing build and was washed over by a cascade of color from the perfectly stained glass reflecting the sun's light into a prism of beauty with the two sides of the cathedral having vastly different colors. The right like cold shades of a glacier with a spray of white, the left a warm array of colors brighter than the sun on a midsummer day. Along with the pillars holding the castle up branching into a natural shape giving one the feeling of being in a forest with the sun shining through translucent leaves. All of it came together to make the cathedral feel natural like it grew into the shape from a young sapling.

I sat down and looked around in wonder during the opening notes of the organ music, cut abruptly by the sight of my mom crying. I’d never seen her cry aside from the time when my grandfather passed away. I asked if she was okay.

“It’s so beautiful,” she said.

I asked her what made it so beautiful to her. She said that she now understood what the guide meant by Gaudí creating the cathedral for all people to enjoy. There were no fancy paintings or murals, but beauty that could be understood and admired by all classes and ages. My mom told me that she felt okay now, and she had tears of joy for the first time in her life. We all calmed down, said our final goodbyes, and left.

As soon as I stepped outside, the beauty of the city struck me. The twinkling headlights, luminescent signs, and birds fluttering back to their homes. I had a filling and delicious dinner of croissants, and then we headed back to our hotel. When I got inside I had a nice refreshing shower and changed into my comfortable pajamas. I wrote in my diary about what I’d seen that day then brushed my teeth and used the restroom. After a long day I then fell asleep in the bed opposite my parents before my head even hit the pillow.
